# AI Career Coach Guide

Workshop note: This document is sample content for the Juntos IBM workshop. It is meant for demo use only and provides guidance on how AI is transforming careers and what skills students should develop.

## Section 1: Understanding AI's Impact on Work

### Three Ways AI Changes Jobs

**1. Automation**: AI replaces repetitive tasks
- Example: Automated data entry, basic scheduling, simple calculations
- Impact: Frees humans for more complex work

**2. Augmentation**: AI helps humans do their jobs better
- Example: Doctors using AI to analyze medical images, writers using AI to check grammar
- Impact: Makes workers more productive and effective

**3. Transformation**: AI creates entirely new types of work
- Example: Prompt engineers, AI trainers, machine learning specialists
- Impact: Opens new career paths that didn't exist before

### The Key Insight

**AI won't replace most jobs—but people who use AI will replace people who don't.**

Your goal: Learn to work WITH AI as a powerful tool in whatever career you choose.

## Section 3: Essential Skills for an AI-Powered Future

### Human Skills That AI Cannot Replace

**1. Critical Thinking**
- Evaluating information quality
- Making decisions with incomplete data
- Questioning assumptions
- Solving novel problems

**2. Creativity**
- Generating truly original ideas
- Combining concepts in new ways
- Artistic vision and expression
- Innovation and invention

**3. Emotional Intelligence**
- Understanding and managing emotions
- Empathy and compassion
- Building relationships
- Reading social situations

**4. Communication**
- Explaining complex ideas simply
- Persuasion and negotiation
- Active listening
- Adapting message to audience

**5. Ethics and Judgment**
- Making moral decisions
- Considering long-term consequences
- Balancing competing values
- Taking responsibility

**6. Leadership**
- Inspiring and motivating others
- Building and managing teams
- Strategic vision
- Conflict resolution

**7. Adaptability**
- Learning new skills quickly
- Adjusting to change
- Resilience in face of challenges
- Openness to new ideas

**8. Domain Expertise**
- Deep knowledge in a specific field
- Understanding context and nuance
- Recognizing patterns and exceptions
- Professional judgment

### Technical Skills to Develop

**Basic Digital Literacy**:
- Using common software and apps
- Online research and evaluation
- Digital communication
- File management and organization

**Data Literacy**:
- Reading charts and graphs
- Understanding statistics basics
- Interpreting data
- Recognizing data quality issues

**AI Literacy**:
- Understanding how AI works
- Knowing when to use AI tools
- Prompt engineering
- Evaluating AI outputs

**Industry-Specific Tools**:
- Learn the AI tools used in your field
- Stay current with new technologies
- Practice using tools effectively
- Understand tool limitations

## Section 4: Educational Pathways

### Four-Year College Degree

**Best for**:
- Careers requiring deep theoretical knowledge
- Professional fields (healthcare, engineering, law)
- Research and academia
- Management and leadership roles

**Advantages**:
- Comprehensive education
- Networking opportunities
- Access to research and resources
- Credential for many careers

**Considerations**:
- Higher cost and time investment
- May include courses not directly career-related
- Requires strong academic preparation

### Community College

**Best for**:
- Cost-effective start to higher education
- Technical and vocational training
- Transfer pathway to four-year schools
- Career changers and adult learners

**Advantages**:
- Lower cost
- Flexible scheduling
- Smaller class sizes
- Strong connections to local employers

**Considerations**:
- May need to transfer for bachelor's degree
- Less residential campus experience
- Fewer research opportunities

### Apprenticeships

**Best for**:
- Skilled trades
- Learning while earning
- Hands-on learners
- Immediate career entry

**Advantages**:
- Paid training
- No student debt
- Direct path to employment
- Practical experience

**Considerations**:
- Limited to certain fields
- Competitive to enter
- May require additional certifications

### Certifications and Bootcamps

**Best for**:
- Specific technical skills
- Career changers
- Supplementing existing education
- Fast entry to tech fields

**Advantages**:
- Focused, practical training
- Shorter time commitment
- Often job-placement assistance
- Industry-recognized credentials

**Considerations**:
- May lack theoretical foundation
- Not all certifications are equally valued
- Requires self-motivation
- May need to be renewed

### Internships and Work Experience

**Best for**:
- Exploring career options
- Building professional network
- Gaining practical experience
- Demonstrating skills to employers

**Advantages**:
- Real-world experience
- Professional connections
- Resume building
- Sometimes leads to job offers

**Considerations**:
- May be unpaid or low-paid
- Competitive to obtain
- Time commitment during school
- Not all provide meaningful experience

## Section 5: Making Career Decisions

### Questions to Ask Yourself

**1. What are my interests?**
- What subjects do I enjoy?
- What activities make me lose track of time?
- What problems do I want to solve?

**2. What are my strengths?**
- What comes naturally to me?
- What do others say I'm good at?
- What skills have I developed?

**3. What are my values?**
- What kind of impact do I want to make?
- What work environment suits me?
- What work-life balance do I need?

**4. What are my constraints?**
- What can I afford for education?
- What family obligations do I have?
- Where do I need or want to live?

**5. What opportunities are available?**
- What careers are growing in my area?
- What education can I access?
- What connections do I have?

### Exploring Career Options

**Research methods**:
- Informational interviews with professionals
- Job shadowing experiences
- Online career resources
- Career assessment tools
- Industry publications and news
- Professional association websites
- LinkedIn profiles of people in the field

**Questions to research**:
- What does a typical day look like?
- What education and training is required?
- What is the salary range?
- What are the job prospects?
- How is AI changing this field?
- What skills are most important?
- What are the advancement opportunities?

### Combining Interests

You don't have to choose just one interest. Many successful careers combine multiple fields:

- **Art + Technology** = UX design, game design, digital marketing
- **Healthcare + Technology** = Health informatics, medical device design
- **Business + Creativity** = Marketing, brand management, entrepreneurship
- **Science + Communication** = Science writing, public health education
- **Engineering + Environment** = Sustainable design, renewable energy
- **Education + Technology** = Educational technology, online learning design

## Section 6: Preparing Now

### In High School

**Academic preparation**:
- Take challenging courses in your areas of interest
- Develop strong writing and communication skills
- Build math and science foundation
- Learn a second language if possible
- Maintain good grades for college/scholarship options

**Skill development**:
- Join clubs related to career interests
- Participate in competitions or projects
- Volunteer in relevant fields
- Take online courses in areas of interest
- Build a portfolio of work

**Exploration**:
- Attend career fairs and college visits
- Talk to professionals in fields you're considering
- Try internships or summer programs
- Research different careers and pathways
- Experiment with AI tools in your areas of interest

**Planning**:
- Research education requirements for careers of interest
- Explore scholarship opportunities
- Understand financial aid options
- Create a timeline for applications
- Build relationships with teachers and mentors for recommendations

### Staying Current

**Follow industry trends**:
- Read news about AI and your field of interest
- Follow professionals on social media
- Subscribe to industry newsletters
- Attend webinars or virtual events
- Join online communities

**Continuous learning**:
- Take advantage of free online courses
- Practice with AI tools
- Learn from YouTube tutorials
- Read books about your field
- Seek feedback and mentorship

**Build your network**:
- Connect with professionals on LinkedIn
- Attend local meetups or events
- Join professional organizations (many have student memberships)
- Maintain relationships with teachers and mentors
- Help others and share what you learn
